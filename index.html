1

Olist E-Commerce Business Analysis

RevoU — June 2026

a. Background Project

Olist operates as a B2C marketplace connecting sellers and customers in Brazil. The company experienced continuous monthly revenue fluctuations, creating operational blindness for the category management, finance, and marketplace operations teams regarding the core drivers of these movements.

b. Objective

To analyze over 100,000 marketplace transaction records from January 2017 to August 2018 using a data-driven approach to discover the true variables behind revenue drops or gains, and design a recurring monitoring dashboard to support targeted business adjustments.

c. Scope / Method

The scope was strictly narrowed to completed delivered orders. The methodology followed problem understanding, systematic data cleaning (handling categorical null entries, verifying types), Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to trace correlations between volume and Average Order Value (AOV), and dashboard visualization deployment.

d. Tools Used

SQL (Google BigQuery) for relational data aggregation, Python (Google Colab) for structured data cleaning, and Tableau for full stack dashboard design.

e. Result / Recommendations

  • Delivered order volume is the strongest visible driver of overall marketplace revenue (peaking in Nov 2017 with 7,289 orders and Rp987.8K revenue). AOV serves as a crucial balancing exception when order counts stagnate.
  • The Top 5 categories drive an intense 39.89% concentration of total marketplace revenue.
  • Action Plan: Recommended that Olist's category management teams safeguard product availability and visibility for the Top 5 contributors. Suggested deploying bundle recommendations and high-value category highlights during volume plateaus (such as April–May 2018) to elevate basket value and protect total revenues.

2

Customer Segmentation Analysis using K-Means Clustering

RevoU — May 2026

a. Background Project

RevoBank needed to improve credit card usage frequency among its existing customer portfolio. The product management and marketing teams lacked empirical client profiles, leading to generic campaigns that failed to balance financial risk parameters like Debt-to-Income (DTI) with active spending habits.

b. Objective

Process and parse 5,531 raw active credit card accounts and 2,000 distinct customer profiles to isolate precise behavior groups via data-driven clustering, allowing RevoBank to deploy highly personalized card usage campaigns and maximize MDR profits safely.

c. Scope / Method

Evaluated cumulative financial behaviors up to the cutoff date of May 31, 2025. Applied rigorous cleaning flows (data type conversions, outlier handling), feature engineering (generating age groups, retirement metrics, and DTI ratios), executed RFM matrix preparation, and deployed a machine learning K-Means Clustering algorithm (optimizing k=4 groups via the Elbow Method).

d. Tools Used

Python inside Google Colab utilizing libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn for mathematical modeling, and Matplotlib/Seaborn for exploratory chart building.

e. Result / Recommendations

  • The data disproved standard assumptions by revealing that retired users maintain a much lower, safer average DTI (0.067) compared to non-retired users (0.296).
  • Clustered 1,195 active users into 4 distinct groups: Premium Active Spenders (66 users), Active Growth Customers (291 users), Risky Moderate Spenders (293 users, highest DTI), and Low Value Mass Customers (545 users).
  • Action Plan: Prioritize high-reward loyalty plans for Premium Active Spenders and spend-based gamification cards for Active Growth Customers. Constrain aggressive limit upgrades for Risky Moderate Spenders due to their volatile 0.27 debt ratio footprint.
